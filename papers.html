The lower bound papers

In this sequence of papers, I look at the intrinsinc complexity of oblivios data structures with a special focus on ORAM and PIR.

Background

  • The first lower bound on ORAMs was given by Goldreich in the first 1987 paper on ORAMs (see also Goldreich and Ostrovsky). The lower bound was in the balls-and-bins model in which each logical block of data was seen as an indivisible and opaque blob of data. And it holds also for the offline case.
  • Boyle and Naor showed that removing the balls-and-bins restriction on the lower bound for the offline case would give a result on sorting circuit complexity.
  • Larsen and Nielsen removed the ball-and-bins restriction for online ORAMs and proved that an ORAM consisting on n b-bit blocks implemented on a server with w-bit words requires slowdown proportional to at least b/w log(n/c).
  • Hubáček, Koucký, Král, and Slívová had the same lower bound with respect to a weaker adversary.

The papers

  • With Kevin Yeo, in Eurocrypt 2019, we obtain the same lower bound for the weaker notion of (ε,δ)-differentially private RAM, for constant ε.
  • With Kevin Yeo and Sarvar Patel, in Crypto 2020, we show the same result for Symmetric Searchable Encryption that leak the decoupled key-equality pattern; i.e., the key-equality leakage between update and query operations is decoupled and the adversary only learns whether two operations of the same type are performed on the same key or not. Note that constant-slowdown SSE is possible if the adversary is allowed to learn whether a pair of read and write operation are performed on the same key.
  • With Kevin Yeo, in Eurocrypt 2023, we provide a framework for proving lower bounds for differentially private and oblivious data structures. As a special case we prove the first non-trivial lower bound for differentially private RAMs that is independent of the size b of the logical blocks. Note that previous lower bound had a b/w multiplicative factor that made the bound trivial when b is much larger than w. A similar result had been obtained by Komargodski and Lin for the stronger notion of Oblivious RAM.
  • With Kevin Yeo, in CRYPTO 2023, we prove that differentially private RAMs have the same slowdown even with respect to a transiente adversary that receives two memory snapshot and the transcript of one operation.
  • With Kevin Yeo, in SODA 22, we prove a lower bound on the time/hint tradeoff of computationally secure PIR with public preprocessing improving the previous result of Beimel, Ishai and Malkin.
